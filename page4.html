Featured National Artist

Pacita Abad

Pacita Abad (1946-2004) was known for many things. She was known developing the unique style called trapunto painting which joined canvas painting with textiles, shells, mirrors and other found materials. Her works were known to be colorful and often depicted the women of color she met during her travels. Her works were vibrant and colorful, as such, her most famous work, painting the Alkaff bridge in Singapore. In her more than 30-year career, She exhibited her work in over 200 museums, galleries and other venues, including 75 solo shows, around the world

Art Works

Layers of Earth (1988)

Layers of Earth is based on a sketch Pacita made while her plane circled the Macchu Picchu Mountain in Peru.

Alkaff Bridge/Art Bridge

Pacita Abad finished painting this bridge in January of 2004, a few months before her passing. The previously dull bridge is now vibrant and colourful painted with unique patterns

Sepoc Wall (1985)

This colourful piece depicts fishes, and colourful plants in a marine environment. She used mirrors and buttons on stitched and padded canvas
